Regional Surges
Models a scenario where one party outperforms their province-wide swing in a specific region — for example, an NDP surge on Vancouver Island on top of whatever province-wide shift you've set in the pie chart. Select a party for each region you want to model, then use the +/− buttons to set the size of the regional boost in percentage points. The surge is drawn proportionally from the other two parties in each affected riding.
Differential Turnout
Models higher or lower enthusiasm among each party's voters. A positive value means more low-propensity voters from that party show up relative to 2024; a negative value means some supporters stay home. The boost is applied as a multiplier to each party's vote share in every riding before the swing is calculated. Based on BC research, turnout effects tend to be larger in Metro Vancouver and smaller in the Interior and North.
